You can see I've been using all my threads. (sorry, that was a nerdy joke üò©).

Growing up without a fast computer nor much resources, I found myself tinkering with low-level programming: game engines, embedded software, eventually a kernel from scratch for Raspberry PI.

And "It works..." is not enough for me. While learning I found industry gurus Yan Chernikov, Jonathan Blow, Casey Muratori, from whom I learned the pillars of good code: simplicity, performance, robustness, maintainability, and documentation.

I discovered my passion for photography while looking for ways to spend my time, that wasn't programming. Eventually, I wondered how can I combine this double life. For me, I know the meaning of life would lie somewhere between engineering and creativity. Although I'm still searching, this page layout (photos and projects) is my take on that.
