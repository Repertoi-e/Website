Growing up without access to a fast computer, I found myself tinkering with low-level programming: game engines, embedded software, and eventually a kernel from scratch for Raspberry PI.

"It works..." has never been enough for me... Launching a program 10s of times, on the brink of going insane, just to measure a potential performance gain of a few milliseconds; commiting a change which deletes a bunch of crufty code; or starting a project from scratch, baking in control, minimizing dependencies, and applying data-oriented design - that's how I roll. I'm proud when I do those things, because I believe that enduring software in the age of computing shouldn't be slow, buggy, undocumented or unmaintable.

My inspiration comes from industry gurus like Yan Chernikov, Jonathan Blow, Casey Muratori, who taught me the craft of software engineering.

The last few months I started taking pictures of everyone and everything, and filming short scenes and videos. For me, the meaning of life would ultimately lie somewhere between engineering and creativity. Although I'm still searching for that, this page layout (photos combined with projects) is my take on that.
